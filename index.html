“The oldest and strongest emotion of mankind is fear, and the oldest and strongest kind of fear is fear of the unknown” - H.P. Lovecraft

H.P. Lovecraft was haunted by graphic images in his dreams of monsters and realms beyond our own. He transformed them into stories. These stories were transformed again into one of the most extensive and horrific universes that human eyes have laid eyes upon.

The Origins

H.P. Lovecraft connects his many works into one large work. The connections within his expansive universe is demonstrated clearly in At the Mountains of Madness.

The main character, William Dyer, narrates about what he sees on the walls of the lost city in the frigid mountains in Antarctica. He speaks of the Great Old Ones and how they came from space and created life on Earth. He mentions how the Great Old Ones made the creatures that we know today, so they could have something to eat.
